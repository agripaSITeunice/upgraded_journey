Kisad Road Filipino‑Chinese Mural

It’s located along Kisad Road, on the wall between the Baguio General Hospital rotunda and Burnham Park (near the children’s playground).The purpose of the mural is symbolic: it represents the relationship between the Filipino and Chinese communities in Baguio aiming for peace, unity, and recognition of the Chinese-Filipino community’s contributions to the city.Best Time to visit?early before traffic builds up so you can enjoy photographing the full stretch of the wall and take a stroll aswell to appreciate it more.

“Biodiversity of Love” Mural – Kennon Road

An artwork located in along Kennon Road in Barangay Camp 7.It spans about 120 feet, and is meant to visually showcase the rich biodiversity of the Cordillera Administrative Region (CAR) representing flora, fauna, and landscapes from provinces like Baguio, Benguet, Ifugao, Mountain Province, Abra, Apayao, and Kalinga. Better visit this before you go to the city!

Calderon Street

Perfecto-Carantes Street (and its twin street Carantes) is a side street at the foot of Session Road — Baguio’s main downtown thoroughfare.The “living street” concept means the road is redesigned as a shared space for pedestrians, cyclists, and light traffic — with traffic calming measures, cobblestone paving, benches, greenery (flower boxes), and other pedestrian-friendly features.

Sunshine Park

Sunshine Park is one of the public parks/open spaces in Baguio City.It has a covered stage (sometimes referred to as the “Sunshine Park stage”) used for cultural presentations, events, and performances.In 2020, local muralists (including artists from groups such as Pasakalye Group of Artists) painted eight “kalasag” (shield-shaped) panels / posts on the stage area of the park. These murals were made as a tribute to front-liners (healthcare workers, volunteers, police, etc.) during the COVID-19 pandemic.

44th sin-agi

This is the 44th mural created by the Sin-Agi Artists group, inaugurated January 31, 2025 in front of the Bishop’s House (near the city cathedral) in Barangay Kabayanihan.The mural features a triptych of scenes: one shows the cathedral with a priest and crowd; another shows men carrying a house; a third depicts young people teaching elders how to use modern gadgets — a symbolic mix of tradition, community spirit, and generational bridging.This is a great mural-hopping route; you’ll discover cafes and small shops along the way!

Tam-Awan Village

Tam-awan Village is a reconstructed indigenous-style village, with authentic traditional Cordilleran huts (from Ifugao and Kalinga), rebuilt and arranged on a hillside to mimic a traditional highland village.Inside the village you’ll find art exhibits (paintings, wood carvings), galleries, cultural workshops (weaving, carving, crafts), craft shops, and sometimes indigenous music/dance presentations or cultural performances.
